# 04 — Client Onboarding

## Objective

Move from inquiry to live pilot in 7 days without drowning in custom setup.

## Onboarding phases

1. inquiry intake
2. discovery / diagnostic call
3. proposal and scope lock
4. access collection
5. workflow build
6. QA and launch
7. first 14-day stabilization

---

## 1. Inquiry intake

### Required intake info

Collect before discovery if possible:

- company / team name
- lead volume
- lead sources
- current CRM
- biggest leakage point
- buyer vs seller mix
- who follows up now
- ideal launch deadline

### Fit screen

Good fit if:

- they actively generate leads
- they admit follow-up leakage
- they can move quickly on access
- one operator can own hot lead follow-up

Bad fit if:

- they want a giant transformation project
- nobody owns follow-up
- lead volume is too low to justify the install
- they expect AI to replace all humans immediately

---

## 2. Discovery / diagnostic call

### Goal

Identify the highest-leakage workflow and define the week-one wedge.

### Questions to ask

- where do leads come from today?
- what happens in the first 10 minutes after a lead arrives?
- what percentage are touched same day?
- how do you define a qualified lead?
- what stages in your CRM are actually trusted?
- what happens to leads who are not ready now?
- what stale database exists?
- who should receive hot leads?
- what compliance constraints matter for text / email?

### Discovery outputs

- chosen wedge
- chosen audience: buyer / seller / investor
- chosen channels: SMS / email / both
- chosen reporting recipients
- approved 7-day scope

---

## 3. Proposal and scope lock

### Scope statement format

The proposal should be one page and concrete.

Include:

- workflow being fixed
- lead sources in scope
- personas in scope
- channels in scope
- what will be live by day 7
- what is explicitly out of scope
- what client must provide by day 2

### Required client deliverables

- CRM/admin access
- sample lead export
- brand voice notes
- approved lead owners
- inbox / number / sending channel access
- calendar or booking preference if booking is included

---

## 4. Access collection

### Access checklist

- CRM admin or sandbox access
- form / portal lead access if needed
- messaging tool access
- analytics or reporting access
- list of users / owners
- timezone / hours of operation
- opt-out language / compliance rules if any

### Data request

Need at least one sample of:

- new inbound lead
- stale lead export
- current stage list
- current follow-up messages if any

---

## 5. Kickoff

### Kickoff agenda

- restate the narrow goal
- confirm week-one workflow
- confirm who owns approvals
- confirm who receives hot leads
- confirm business hour SLA
- confirm launch date

### Kickoff deliverables from ResultEstate

- workflow map
- CRM stage map
- first-pass message scripts
- launch timeline
- decision log

---

## 6. Stabilization after launch

For 14 days after launch:

- monitor routing accuracy
- monitor delivery / reply rates
- tune qualification prompts
- tighten stage hygiene
- review missed handoffs
- publish daily summary

## Client success standard

The client should feel in control by the end of week two.

If only the builder understands the system, the onboarding failed.
